Abstract

All-in-one image restoration models can restore high-quality images from various types and levels of degradation in one single model without prior information about the degradation type. In this work, we focus on improving both the performance and efficiency of the all-in-one image restoration model. First, to capture both global and local information crucial for various tasks, we design a transformer block with alternating channel self-attention and spatial self-attention. Second, recognizing that plain image regions are easier to restore than detailed ones, and that different tasks have different levels of difficulty, we propose to allocate resources dynamically based on the content and task of the image. Specifically, we design attention mixer modules that automatically choose complex self-attention for detailed areas and hard tasks, and simple squeeze-and-excitation for plain areas and easy tasks. Our method achieves state-of-the-art results on several restoration tasks, including image denoising, deraining, and dehazing, with fewer FLOPs compared to previous works.
